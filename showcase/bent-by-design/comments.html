Design Showcase

Bent by Design

Furniture Design Studio
by
Professor Ravi Mokashi Punekar
Department of Design, IIT Guwahati

Comments and Feedback:


Design works from Bent by design fall under three categories:

  • Exhibition and Space Design
  • urniture Design and
  • Design Installations

Their projects include:
  • 'Disappear' Exhibition / Installation of the Adivasi communities: Client: Equation, Bengaluru.
    Brief: It was an exhibition/installation titled 'Disappear' which was a visual expression of the realities and context of the 'Adivasi' (tribal) communities. Project work included designing the exhibition panels, posters, brochures and media related documents. A significant requirement of this project was the design of a knock down and lightweight paneling system for this exhibition that was to travel to many different locations.
  • Mobile Lighting Installation:
    Brief: A turnkey project commissioned to Bent by design to install a large mobile lamp in the foyer of an office. The complete design assignment involved concept to design realization including fabrication of large aluminum dishes had to be hand beaten to build the shades and its onsite installation.
  • Installation of an electro-mechanical installation for the Sadhu Vaswani Museum, Pune:
    Brief: The installation depicts the saint Sadhu Vaswani journeying on a boat on the Hoogly River through a series of 16 foot long waves, which move to and fro in respect to each other to create a wave pattern. Boats are attached to the waves, which move along it with the help of motors and proximity sensors.
  • View this installation in action at:
    http://bent-by-design.blogspot.com/2011/08/final-show-and-behind-scene-of-boat-in_29.html
  • Design and Execution of Customer Experience Centers: Client: Wipro, Bengaluru.
  • Design of Retail Space and fixture design.
  • Design of Exhibition.
  • Design of Office spaces.
  • Design of Home Interiors.
  • Design of Customized Hardwood Furniture for homes, individuals and institutions.

Amongst their recent projects they have been making home furniture for individuals and families. As seen the design uses traditional carpentry methods using teak wood for the wall cabinets that hang from the much celebrated 'shaker pegs' on the wall. These 'shaker pegs' run all along the wall length so that one can also hang other kitchen accessories like pots, pans and veggie bags.

'Rushi' furniture range - a second project for the family, involves making a study desk with chair and a book-shelf for children.
